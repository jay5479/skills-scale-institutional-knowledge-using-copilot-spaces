# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Quality Assurance / QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads own the testing strategy, quality standards, and acceptance validation. They partner with developers and product managers to ensure features meet acceptance criteria and quality gates before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain test plans and quality standards
- Establish QA approach (manual, automation, E2E, unit test coverage)
- Review acceptance criteria and raise clarification questions
- Lead acceptance testing and sign-off on features
- Identify and log defects, prioritize rework
- Collaborate on post-release smoke tests and rollback procedures

### Goals
- Minimize production defects and rework
- Reduce cycle time for QA without sacrificing quality
- Catch issues early in the development process

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning (QA approach discussion)
- PR reviews (test coverage feedback)
- End-of-sprint review / demo (acceptance validation)
- Risk registers and release checklists

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Partner on test automation, code review feedback for testability
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on acceptance criteria clarity and feature prioritization
- **Project Managers**: Report on quality metrics and testing blockers
- **Technical Lead**: Advise on test architecture and automation strategy

---

## Technical Lead / Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Leads guide architectural decisions, conduct design reviews, and ensure solutions align with system-wide technical strategy. They balance short-term delivery with long-term maintainability.

### Responsibilities
- Review technical designs and propose architectural improvements
- Identify technical risks and mitigation strategies
- Mentor developers on design patterns and best practices
- Conduct code reviews for complex or high-impact changes
- Advise on performance, scalability, and security concerns

### Goals
- Ensure solutions are scalable, maintainable, and resilient
- Share knowledge and raise team technical capability
- Reduce technical debt and rework

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews
- Code review comments
- Risk registers (technical risks)
- Retrospectives and lessons learned

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provide mentorship, design feedback, and technical guidance
- **Project Managers**: Escalate technical risks and dependency concerns
- **Security Engineer**: Collaborate on threat modeling and security architecture
- **QA Lead**: Advise on test architecture and automation approach

---

## Scrum Master / Iteration Facilitator

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters (or Iteration Facilitators) manage team processes, remove blockers, and ensure ceremonies are effective. They coach teams on agile practices and help resolve impediments.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate standups, planning, and retrospective meetings
- Identify and escalate blockers to the PM
- Track sprint velocity and help team improve predictability
- Coach team on agile practices and continuous improvement
- Maintain project board health and backlog clarity

### Goals
- Maximize team flow and minimize context-switching
- Build a high-performing, self-organizing team
- Reduce time spent on process overhead

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups (facilitation and blocker tracking)
- Sprint planning and retrospectives
- Weekly PM syncs (impediments and velocity trends)

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate on blockers, escalations, and velocity trends
- **Development Team**: Coach on agile practices and help remove impediments
- **Product Manager**: Ensure backlog clarity and prioritization alignment

---

## Stakeholder / Sponsor

### Role Summary
Sponsors provide business context, approve scope and trade-offs, and communicate project status to leadership. They ensure the project aligns with organizational strategy and remove business barriers.

### Responsibilities
- Define business objectives and success metrics
- Approve project scope, timeline, and resource allocation
- Attend kickoff and milestone reviews
- Make trade-off decisions when conflicts arise
- Communicate project status to leadership and customers
- Support escalations and remove business blockers

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers measurable business value
- Maintain stakeholder confidence and alignment
- Enable rapid decision-making

### Typical Communication
- Kick-off meeting
- Monthly stakeholder updates or milestone reviews
- Ad-hoc escalations for decisions
- Release announcements

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Receive escalations and trade-off decisions
- **Product Manager**: Provide business context and prioritization guidance
- **Development Team**: Visibility into progress and critical decisions

---

## UX/Design Lead

### Role Summary
UX/Design Leads define user experience, conduct research, and guide design decisions. They advocate for usability and ensure solutions meet user needs and accessibility standards.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design systems guidance
- Collaborate with product and engineering on trade-offs
- Review implementations against design specs
- Ensure accessibility compliance (WCAG standards)

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, user-centric solutions
- Reduce support burden and user friction
- Maintain design consistency across products

### Typical Communication
- Discovery and planning meetings
- Design reviews and feedback sessions
- Acceptance criteria review (usability aspects)
- Retrospectives (user feedback learnings)

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Partner on user research and feature prioritization
- **Developers**: Collaborate on implementation trade-offs and design feedback
- **QA Lead**: Define usability and accessibility acceptance criteria
- **Project Manager**: Provide design timeline estimates and dependency tracking

---

## Security Engineer

### Role Summary
Security Engineers ensure that projects meet compliance requirements, reduce security risks, and build secure-by-design solutions. They partner with developers and leads on threat modeling and incident response.

### Responsibilities
- Participate in design and threat modeling sessions
- Conduct security code reviews and vulnerability scanning
- Ensure compliance with organizational security policies
- Lead security incident response and post-mortems
- Advise on authentication, data protection, and access controls

### Goals
- Minimize security vulnerabilities and data risks
- Ensure compliance with regulations and organizational standards
- Build a culture of security-conscious development

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews (security considerations)
- Code review feedback (security best practices)
- Risk registers (security risks)
- Incident response and post-mortems
- Release checklists (security sign-off)

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Technical Lead**: Collaborate on threat modeling and secure architecture
- **Developers**: Provide security code review feedback and best practices
- **Project Manager**: Report security risks and compliance requirements
- **QA Lead**: Define security acceptance criteria and testing approach
